# Sven König

Sven König is a media artist whose early-2000s work helped establish video
glitch and datamosh-adjacent techniques as a deliberate artistic practice
rather than an accident to be hidden. His piece *Browser Based* is
frequently cited in glitch art circles alongside other early digital-decay
and compression-artifact works from that era.

## Why he matters here

Before dedicated "datamosh" software and tutorials existed (the YouTube
tutorial wave referenced in the main README is roughly 2012-2015), a
smaller scene of media artists were already treating codec failure —
broken keyframes, corrupted motion compensation, encoding artifacts — as
raw material. König's work is part of that lineage: deliberately exposing
the *process* of digital video (encoding, compression, decay) as the
subject of the piece, rather than a transparent pipe to content.

## Notes for further research

This reference file is intentionally a starting point rather than a
complete biography — if you're building on this lineage, primary sources
worth tracking down include:
- Direct artist statements/interviews about working method (hex editing?
  codec manipulation? hardware glitching?).

  showed this work).
- Comparisons with contemporaries working in the same "compression as
  medium" space (e.g. the broader databending/glitch art movement that
  also bent image and audio formats, not just video).
